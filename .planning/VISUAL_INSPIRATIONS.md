# Visual Inspirations — Room & Interior Design References

These are the pixel art games we're drawing from for PrivacyQuest's room layouts, furniture placement, and overall "lived-in" feel. Each game solves a specific problem we face.

---

## Pokemon Center Interiors (Gen 3: FireRed/LeafGreen, Ruby/Sapphire)

**What to steal: Functional clarity.**

Every object in a Pokemon Center signals its purpose. The healing counter is the focal point — always center-front, always the first thing you see entering. PCs line the walls. Potted plants fill dead corners. Walkways are obvious. No furniture is random.

**Design rules extracted:**
- Counter = barrier between player and service NPC (receptionist desk, check-in counter)
- Machines/equipment against walls, never floating mid-room
- Open center for walking — rooms breathe
- Every prop either "does something" or "fills dead space with purpose"

**Reference links:**
- [Spriters Resource — FireRed/LeafGreen Tileset](https://www.spriters-resource.com/game_boy_advance/pokemonfireredleafgreen/asset/3870/) — Complete interior tileset rip
- [Spriters Resource — FireRed/LeafGreen Full Assets](https://www.spriters-resource.com/game_boy_advance/pokemonfireredleafgreen/) — Buildings, tilesets, maps
- [Game UI Database — Ruby/Sapphire/Emerald](https://www.gameuidatabase.com/gameData.php?id=1708) — Gameplay screenshots with UI + room layouts

**Applicable rooms:** Reception (front desk focal point), Hospital Entrance (welcome kiosk as anchor)

## EarthBound (Mother 2) — Hospital & Town Interiors

**What to steal: Personality through clutter.**

EarthBound rooms feel lived-in because objects are slightly asymmetric. A trash can next to a desk. A plant that's a little too big. A poster on the wall that doesn't need to be there. Nothing is perfectly symmetrical — and that's what makes it feel like a real place, not a furniture grid.

**Design rules extracted:**
- Slight asymmetry = lived-in (one extra chair, a plant in only ONE corner)
- Wall decorations at eye-level, spaced naturally (not every 4 tiles)
- Personality props that don't serve gameplay but serve worldbuilding
- Oblique perspective handles desks/beds with character

**Reference links:**
- [Spriters Resource — Twoson Interior Tileset](https://www.spriters-resource.com/snes/earthbound/sheet/147616/) — Full interior sprite sheet including hospital, shops, homes
- [Spriters Resource — EarthBound Full Collection](https://www.spriters-resource.com/snes/earthbound/) — All tilesets, overworlds, interiors
- [EarthBound Wiki — Twoson](https://earthbound.fandom.com/wiki/Twoson) — Hospital and town layout context

**Applicable rooms:** Break Room (personal items, asymmetric seating), Lab (quirky equipment placement)

## Chrono Trigger — Tech/Medical Interiors

**What to steal: Density and atmosphere.**

Chrono Trigger fills rooms with MORE objects than you'd expect — crates stacked in corners, pipes along walls, consoles at angles. The future domes (Arris Dome, Proto Dome) are basically tech-medical facilities. Empty floor space is intentionally limited. Rooms feel rich because every tile has texture.

**Design rules extracted:**
- Fill corners with stacked objects, pipes, equipment — no empty corners
- Cable/pipe runs along walls create visual continuity between furniture clusters
- Terminals and consoles at different heights create visual variety
- Cluttered inventor workspaces (Lucca's house) are the gold standard for IT Office

**Reference links:**
- [SNESMaps — Chrono Trigger Full Map Selection](https://www.snesmaps.com/maps/ChronoTrigger/ChronoTriggerMapSelect.html) — Complete labeled interior maps. Key locations:
  - **Arris Dome** (2300 AD) — post-apocalyptic tech/medical dome with consoles, beds, terminals
  - **Guardia Castle** — large rooms that feel full despite being tile-based
  - **Lucca's House** — cluttered inventor's workshop (IT Office reference)
- [Spriters Resource — Chrono Trigger](https://www.spriters-resource.com/snes/chronotrigger/) — Full sprite sheets and tilesets
- [Chrono Compendium — Arris Dome](https://www.chronocompendium.com/Term/Arris_Dome.html) — Layout context and writeup

**Applicable rooms:** IT Office (dense tech equipment, cable runs), Records Room (filing density)

## Stardew Valley — Harvey's Clinic (Most Directly Relevant)

**What to steal: Warmth and approachability in a medical space.**

Harvey's Clinic is literally a pixel art medical office. It has: examination table, medical posters, a plant, a bookshelf, a waiting area with seats facing the counter. It's a clinic that feels like a real place, not a grid of medical sprites. The modded "Interiors of Pelican Town" redesign adds even more medical equipment and better furniture arrangement.

**Design rules extracted:**
- Medical spaces need warmth: plants, personal items, non-clinical colors
- Waiting area with seats FACING the counter (not random chair placement)
- Examination room separated from reception by furniture barriers
- Wall decorations (posters, certificates, signs) at logical positions
- Each room needs a focal point: counter, big machine, central table

**Reference links:**
- [Stardew Valley Wiki — Harvey's Clinic](https://stardewvalleywiki.com/Harvey%27s_Clinic) — Official wiki with interior layout. Single best reference for our hospital rooms.
- [NexusMods — Interiors of Pelican Town (Clinic)](https://www.nexusmods.com/stardewvalley/mods/17360) — Fan redesign showing how the clinic *should* look
- [Stardew Valley Wiki — Harvey](https://stardewvalleywiki.com/Harvey) — Schedule reveals walkable space design

**Applicable rooms:** ER (examination area), Reception (waiting room layout), all rooms (warmth through plants/personal items)

## Cross-Game Design Cheat Sheet

Use this when placing furniture in any room:

| Problem | Fix | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Desks placed randomly | Furniture against walls, counters create zones | Pokemon |
| Bulletin boards everywhere | Wall decorations at eye-level, spaced naturally | EarthBound |
| Room feels empty/sparse | Fill corners with stacked objects, cable runs, equipment | Chrono Trigger |
| No sense of purpose | Each room needs a focal point (counter, big machine, central table) | Stardew |
| Everything symmetrical | Slight asymmetry = lived-in. One extra chair. A plant in only one corner. | EarthBound |
| Feels clinical/cold | Add plants, personal items (coffee mugs, headphones), warm colors | Stardew |
| No visual flow | Cable trays, floor patterns, or furniture lines guide the eye | Chrono Trigger |
| Can't tell rooms apart | Each room needs a unique color palette and signature prop | All four |
