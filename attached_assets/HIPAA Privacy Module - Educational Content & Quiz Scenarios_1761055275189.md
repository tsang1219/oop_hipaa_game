# **HIPAA Privacy Module \- Educational Content & Quiz Scenarios**

## **Game Context**

**Setting:** Startup General Hospital \- a chaotic blend of tech startup energy and healthcare reality **Tone:** Out of Pocket style \- snarky but compliant, fun but accurate **Your Role:** New Privacy Officer trying to survive your first week

---

## **Educational Components (5 Interactable Items)**

### **📋 Item 1: "Welcome Manual" (On your desk)**

**Title:** So You're Responsible for PHI Now (Good Luck\!)

**Content:** "Welcome to healthcare, where Protected Health Information (PHI) is more valuable than Bitcoin and twice as regulated.

**PHI \= The Big 18 Identifiers \+ Health Info** Think: names, addresses, SSNs, medical records, dates of birth, phone numbers, photos, basically anything that could identify a patient AND their health status.

**The Golden Rule:** If you can connect health data to a specific person, congrats—it's PHI. Treat it like it's your own embarrassing medical history, because the penalties for leaking it are... substantial.

**Quick Reality Check:**

* ✅ Discussing Patient 12847's care in a private office \= Fine  
* ❌ Discussing "Karen from Room 302's weird rash" in the elevator \= HIPAA violation speedrun

*Privacy Principle Unlocked: Understanding PHI*"

---

### **🖼️ Item 2: Breakroom Poster \- "Minimum Necessary: It's Not a Suggestion"**

**Title:** Stop. Oversharing.

**Content:** "Look, we get it. Healthcare is gossipy. But HIPAA has opinions about your oversharing habits.

**The Minimum Necessary Rule:** Only access, use, or disclose the LEAST amount of PHI needed to get the job done. Not the most interesting amount. Not the juiciest amount. The MINIMUM.

**Real Talk Examples:**

* **Billing needs:** Patient name, DOB, insurance info ✅  
* **Billing needs:** Full medical history, lab results, therapy notes ❌  
* **Scheduling needs:** Name, contact info, appointment type ✅  
* **Scheduling needs:** Diagnosis, medication list, family drama ❌

**The Test:** Ask yourself "Do I NEED this to do my actual job, or do I just WANT to know?"

If it's want? Close the chart. Walk away. Get a hobby.

*Privacy Principle Unlocked: Minimum Necessary*"

---

### **💻 Item 3: Computer Screen \- "Patient Rights 101"**

**Title:** Patients Have Rights (And They Will Use Them)

**Content:** "Under HIPAA, patients aren't just bodies in beds—they have actual power. Wild, right?

**The Big 5 Patient Rights:**

**1\. Right to Access Their Records** Patients can request their medical records. You have 30 days to comply. They can also ask for electronic copies because it's 2025, not 1995\.

**2\. Right to Request Amendments** "Actually, I don't have diabetes, you have the wrong person." They can request corrections. You don't have to agree, but you have to consider it.

**3\. Right to an Accounting of Disclosures** Patients can ask "who've you been telling about my health?" You need receipts for the last 6 years. Fun times.

**4\. Right to Request Restrictions** They can ask you to NOT share certain info with certain people. You don't have to agree (except for self-pay situations), but they can ask.

**5\. Right to Confidential Communications** "Don't call my house phone, my spouse will answer." Reasonable requests must be honored.

**Bottom Line:** Patients own their health data. We're just the janitors managing it.

*Privacy Principle Unlocked: Patient Rights*"

---

### **📞 Item 4: Whiteboard \- "When You CAN Share PHI (Without Going to Jail)"**

**Title:** Permitted Disclosures: Your Get-Out-of-Jail-Free Card

**Content:** "HIPAA isn't trying to STOP healthcare—it just wants you to be thoughtful about it. Here's when you can share PHI without patient authorization:

**Treatment, Payment, Operations (TPO) \- The Holy Trinity:**

* **Treatment:** Coordinating care between providers ✅  
* **Payment:** Billing insurance, collecting copays ✅  
* **Operations:** Quality improvement, training, audits ✅

**Other Legit Reasons:**

* **Required by Law:** Court orders, mandatory reporting (child abuse, gunshot wounds, certain diseases)  
* **Public Health:** Disease outbreak reporting, FDA recalls  
* **Law Enforcement:** With a warrant, or limited info for locating a suspect  
* **Deceased Persons:** Can share with coroners, funeral directors  
* **Emergencies:** If patient is incapacitated and it's in their best interest

**The Catch:** Even when permitted, still use MINIMUM NECESSARY. Don't word-vomit the entire chart just because you legally could.

*Privacy Principle Unlocked: Permitted Disclosures*"

---

### **🚨 Item 5: Emergency Exit Sign \- "When to Say NO (And Save Your Job)"**

**Title:** The Art of the HIPAA Shutdown

**Content:** "Not everyone who asks for PHI gets it. Sometimes your job is to be the bouncer at Club Medical Records.

**Automatic NO Situations:**

**❌ Family Members Without Authorization** "I'm his mother\!" Cool story. Got written consent? No? Then no chart access.

**❌ Employers (Usually)** Your boss calling to check if you're "really sick"? They get a work note, not your diagnosis. Exception: Workers' comp situations.

**❌ Marketing Without Authorization** Can't sell patient lists to pharmaceutical companies for fun and profit. Shocking, we know.

**❌ Anyone Via Unsecured Communication** "Can you text me the lab results?" Nope. "Email them?" Not without encryption. "Fax?" ...Okay actually fax is somehow still HIPAA-compliant. Don't ask us why.

**The Script:** "I'm unable to confirm or deny any patient information without proper authorization. Here's the form you need to fill out."

**Power Move:** Saying no to inappropriate requests is literally your job. Practice your best "HIPAA says no" face in the mirror.

*Privacy Principle Unlocked: When to Deny Access*"

## **Quiz Scenarios (5 NPC Encounters)**

### **🎭 NPC 1: "Chatty Cathy" \- The Cafeteria Gossip**

**Character:** Radiology Tech, loves drama, means well but has zero filter

**Scenario Setup:** *Cathy leans in conspiratorially at lunch*

"OMG, did you hear about the patient in Room 405? Apparently he came in with a—"

**Question:** What's your move?

**A)** Let her finish, then say "Yeah I heard about that too\!" (Build rapport)

**B)** Interrupt: "Stop. We can't discuss patients by room number in public areas."

**C)** Say nothing and just eat your sad desk salad faster

**D)** Change the subject: "Hey, did you see that email about parking?"

**Correct Answer:** **B** \- Interrupt and shut it down

**Explanation:** Room numbers \+ public spaces \= HIPAA violation combo. Minimum Necessary doesn't apply when the necessary amount is ZERO because you're in a cafeteria.

Option A makes you complicit. Option C enables bad behavior. Option D is passive and doesn't address the violation.

**Feedback if Wrong:** "Your silence just made you an accessory to a HIPAA violation. The cafeteria has ears, and one of them might be that patient's lawyer. Intervene early, intervene politely, but INTERVENE."

**Points:** Correct \= \+15 | Wrong \= \-10 Privacy Meter

---

### **👨‍👩‍👧 NPC 2: "Concerned Husband" \- Family Member Information Request**

**Character:** Well-meaning spouse, no written authorization, genuinely worried

**Scenario Setup:** *A man approaches the nurses' station*

"Hi, I'm John Smith's husband. He was admitted yesterday and I'm at work—can you just tell me if his test results came back? I can't get him on the phone and I'm worried sick."

**Question:** What do you do?

**A)** "He's stable, tests are pending. I'll have him call you when he wakes up."

**B)** "I cannot confirm or deny any patient information without authorization."

**C)** "Let me verify you're on his authorized contact list first."

**D)** Pull up the chart to "just check" if husband is listed as emergency contact

**Correct Answer:** **C** \- Check the authorized contact list

**Explanation:** HIPAA allows disclosure to "persons involved in care" BUT you need to verify the relationship and that the patient hasn't restricted this person. Check your system for authorized contacts or emergency contacts first.

Option A violates privacy by assuming authorization. Option B is technically correct but needlessly robotic—we can share if authorized. Option D means you're already accessing the chart before verification.

**Feedback if Wrong:** "That patient might be in the hospital BECAUSE of this 'husband.' Domestic violence, estranged relationships, and legal separations exist. Always verify before sharing. Two minutes of checking could prevent a lifetime of trauma."

**Points:** Correct \= \+15 | Wrong \= \-15 Privacy Meter

### **📠 NPC 3: "Fax Machine Freddy" \- The Wrong Number Incident**

**Character:** You. This scenario is about YOU finding the mistake.

**Scenario Setup:** *You realize you just faxed discharge paperwork with full medical history, medications, and diagnosis to 555-0123 instead of 555-0132*

"Oh no. Oh no no no. That's... that's Dr. Peterson's rival practice. They're going to see EVERYTHING."

**Question:** What's your first move?

**A)** Call the wrong number immediately, explain the error, request they destroy the fax

**B)** Don't tell anyone and hope they just throw it away

**C)** Tell your supervisor but wait to see if the other office calls you first

**D)** Document the incident and notify your Privacy Officer immediately

**Correct Answer:** **D** \- Document and notify Privacy Officer ASAP

**Explanation:** This is a BREACH. Unsecured PHI went to an unauthorized recipient. Your Privacy Officer needs to assess if it meets the threshold for reporting to HHS and patient notification (more than 500 people \= media notification too, but this sounds like one patient).

Option A is good but not enough. Option B is career suicide. Option C delays mitigation.

**The Real Steps:**

1. Document everything (time, what was sent, to whom)  
2. Notify Privacy Officer  
3. Attempt to retrieve/destroy the information  
4. Conduct risk assessment  
5. Possibly notify the patient

**Feedback if Wrong:** "Congrats, you just turned a small mistake into a reportable breach with potential fines. The cover-up is always worse than the crime. HIPAA has a whole process for this—use it."

**Points:** Correct \= \+20 | Wrong \= \-20 Privacy Meter

### **👔 NPC 4: "Boss Man Brad" \- Employer Medical Information Request**

**Character:** Your company's HR director, thinks he's entitled to everything

**Scenario Setup:** *Brad from HR calls*

"Hey, Jessica called in sick again. Third time this month. Can you pull her records and tell me what's actually going on? I need to know if this is legit or if she's just hungover."

**Question:** Your response?

**A)** "Sure, let me pull her chart..." (He's HR, he's allowed to know, right?)

**B)** "Jessica can provide a fitness-for-duty note, but I can't share her medical information."

**C)** "Let me check with Jessica first and see if she'll authorize me to share."

**D)** "I can tell you dates of service but not diagnosis or treatment details."

**Correct Answer:** **B** \- Fitness-for-duty note only

**Explanation:** Employers are NOT entitled to employee medical information under HIPAA. They can require a doctor's note confirming the employee can/can't work, but diagnosis and treatment details are off-limits.

This is a trap. Brad has zero right to Jessica's chart, even if he signs your paycheck.

**Feedback if Wrong:** "You just violated a coworker's privacy to appease HR. Jessica's medical information is HERS, not the company's. Employers get work status, not diagnoses. Now Jessica can sue both you AND Brad. Congrats\!"

**Points:** Correct \= \+15 | Wrong \= \-15 Privacy Meter

### **🎬 NPC 5: "Celebrity Crisis" \- The FINAL BOSS**

**Character:** Hospital Administrator, panicking about a VIP patient

**Scenario Setup:** *Your boss bursts into your office*

"Code VIP\! We have a celebrity in the ER—reality TV star, you'd know the name. Press is already circling. I need you to:

1. Pull the chart and brief our PR team on the situation  
2. Make sure all staff know to keep it quiet  
3. Move the patient to a private wing ASAP

This is huge for the hospital's reputation. Handle it."

**Question:** What's the priority order of your response?

**A)**

1. Brief PR team with medical details  
2. Alert staff to maintain confidentiality  
3. Coordinate private wing transfer

**B)**

1. Verify patient wants privacy protections  
2. Alert staff to maintain confidentiality  
3. Coordinate private wing transfer (if patient agrees)

**C)**

1. Alert staff to maintain confidentiality  
2. Coordinate private wing transfer  
3. Politely tell PR they get nothing without authorization

**D)**

1. Call legal department  
2. Put out a press release saying no comment  
3. Fire anyone who leaks information

**Correct Answer:** **C** \- Staff alert → Private transfer → Shut down PR

**Explanation:** Celebrity or not, HIPAA applies equally. Actually, celebrities are MORE at risk of inappropriate access (employee snooping).

**The Priority:**

1. **Staff confidentiality reminder:** Send immediate alert that any unauthorized access will be termination \+ criminal charges  
2. **Patient safety:** Move to private area if it helps their care/safety  
3. **PR lockdown:** Zero information without written authorization. Not even "yes they're here."

Option A violates privacy by briefing PR. Option B wastes time on patient preference when confidentiality is always the default. Option D is chaotic evil.

**The Reality:** Every hospital employee who accesses that chart without a treatment reason is committing a HIPAA violation. The audit logs will show who looked, and someone WILL leak it. Your job is damage control.

**Feedback if Wrong:** "You just made the hospital the next healthcare privacy scandal on Twitter. 'Hospital Leaks Celebrity Medical Info' is not the press you want. Celebrity patients are HIPAA stress tests—most organizations fail. Don't be most organizations."

**Points:** Correct \= \+25 | Wrong \= \-25 Privacy Meter

## **Implementation Notes for Game**

**Item Collection Flow:**

1. User must interact with all 5 educational items before any NPC will "activate"  
2. Each item adds a colored icon to the top progress bar  
3. After 5/5 items collected, NPCs get exclamation marks and become interactable

**NPC Unlock Order:**

* NPCs 1-4 can be completed in any order (open world exploration)  
* NPC 5 (Celebrity Crisis) only unlocks after completing 4/5 previous scenarios  
* Each completed NPC gets a green checkmark

**Fail State Mechanic:**

* Wrong answers deplete Privacy Meter  
* If meter hits 0 mid-game, immediate game over screen  
* Can retry from beginning (items remain taught, but scenarios reset)

**Tone Consistency:**

* Educational items: Informative but snarky  
* NPC dialogue: Realistic workplace scenarios  
* Feedback: Direct but not mean (we're teaching, not roasting)
